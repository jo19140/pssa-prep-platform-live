Screen 4 · Vocabulary in context Part 3 of 5
Read the sentence. What does the underlined word mean?

The hikers were reluctant to cross the rickety bridge, but the trail on the other side was the only way home.

Why this item type matters →

Vocab-in-context items measure depth (does the student infer meaning from surrounding text?) rather than memorized definitions. Tier 2 words like reluctant, persuade, linger are the highest-leverage targets for striving readers — common in academic text, rare in conversation.

"It's just rain," her grandmother said, but Mara noticed she did not look up from her sewing. Her stitches had become small and careful, the way they always did when something was wrong.

"Get the flashlight," she said. "And the radio. The one with the hand crank."

Design notes for review

  • Modality coverage: the diagnostic samples each strand of the Simple View of Reading + Scarborough's Rope — word recognition, decoding, vocab, language comprehension. PSSA item types are not required here; this is a literacy screener.
  • Audio + recording: read-aloud on every screen and voice capture on decoding items are non-negotiable for kids who can't yet read instructions on their own. This is the part that opens the market outside PA.
  • No level numbers to the kid. Grade equivalents, Lexile bands, etc. live on the adult dashboard. The kid sees strengths and goals in plain language.
  • Adaptive sequencing: the screener should branch — if a student fails 3 of 5 nonsense words, drop to single-syllable decoding before continuing. (Not visualized; behavior note.)
