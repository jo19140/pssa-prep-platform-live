## pssa_item_g3_moy_conv_d111_word_function

EC: E03.D.1.1.1
Target: word function in a sentence / word_function
The students carried the model ___ across the room.

- A: careful — adjective_used_where_adverb_needed — Careful is an adjective, but the sentence needs an adverb to tell how the students carried the model.
- B (KEY): carefully — correct — Carefully is an adverb that tells how the students carried the model.
- C: care — noun_or_base_verb_used_where_adverb_needed — Care can be a noun or verb, but it does not tell how the action was done.
- D: carefulness — ness_noun_used_where_adverb_needed — Carefulness is a noun, not an adverb that describes the carrying.

## pssa_item_g3_moy_conv_d114_irregular_verb

EC: E03.D.1.1.4
Target: regular and irregular verbs / regular_irregular_verbs
Yesterday, Ava ___ her bicycle.

- A: rided — regular_ed_added_to_irregular_verb — Rided incorrectly adds -ed to the irregular verb ride.
- B: ridden — past_participle_used_as_simple_past — Ridden is a past participle and does not work alone as the simple past verb here.
- C: rides — present_tense_used_for_past_time — Rides is present tense, but yesterday signals past time.
- D (KEY): rode — correct — Rode is the correct simple past form of ride.

## pssa_item_g3_moy_conv_d115_verb_tense

EC: E03.D.1.1.5
Target: simple verb tense / verb_tense
Yesterday, the class ___ across the bridge.

- A (KEY): crossed — correct — Crossed is the correct past-tense verb for the time clue yesterday.
- B: crosses — present_tense_used_for_past_time — Crosses is present tense, but the sentence is about yesterday.
- C: will cross — future_tense_used_for_past_time — Will cross is future tense, but yesterday signals past time.
- D: crossing — ing_form_without_helping_verb — Crossing needs a helping verb to work as the verb in this sentence.

## pssa_item_g3_moy_conv_d116_agreement

EC: E03.D.1.1.6
Target: subject-verb agreement / subject_verb_agreement
Today, the group of students ___ ready to begin.

- A: were — plural_past_verb_agrees_with_nearby_noun — Were incorrectly agrees with students instead of the subject group.
- B: are — plural_present_verb_agrees_with_nearby_noun — Are incorrectly agrees with students instead of the singular subject group.
- C (KEY): is — correct — Is agrees with the singular subject group.
- D: be — base_form_with_no_subject_agreement — Be does not agree with the subject in this sentence.

## pssa_item_g3_moy_conv_d118_conjunctions

EC: E03.D.1.1.8
Target: coordinating and subordinating conjunctions / conjunctions
The path was muddy. Maya wore boots ___ she did not want mud on her socks.

- A (KEY): because — correct — Because correctly shows why Maya wore boots.
- B: although — contrast_conjunction_where_cause_needed — Although shows contrast, but the sentence needs a reason.
- C: or — choice_conjunction_where_cause_needed — Or shows a choice, but the sentence explains the cause.
- D: so — result_conjunction_reverses_cause_effect — So would make the second idea sound like a result, but it is the reason.

## pssa_item_g3_moy_conv_d121_title_caps

EC: E03.D.1.2.1
Target: capitalization in titles / title_capitalization
The class read the book ___.

- A: the secret in the attic — all_title_words_lowercase — The important words in the title should be capitalized.
- B: The Secret In The Attic — small_words_over_capitalized — The small words in and the should not both be capitalized in this title.
- C (KEY): The Secret in the Attic — correct — The first word and important words are capitalized, while in and the stay lowercase.
- D: The secret in the Attic — inconsistent_title_capitalization — Secret should be capitalized as an important word in the title.

## pssa_item_g3_moy_conv_d123_dialogue

EC: E03.D.1.2.3
Target: quotation marks and commas in dialogue / dialogue_punctuation
___ said Omar.

- A: "Please close the gate" — missing_comma_before_dialogue_tag — A comma is needed before the dialogue tag said Omar.
- B (KEY): "Please close the gate," — correct — The comma belongs inside the closing quotation mark before the dialogue tag.
- C: "Please close the gate", — comma_outside_quotation_marks — The comma should be inside the quotation marks.
- D: Please close the gate, — missing_quotation_marks — The spoken words need quotation marks.

## pssa_item_g3_moy_conv_d125_spelling

EC: E03.D.1.2.5
Target: grade-level spelling and suffixes / spelling_in_context
Lena is ___ a poster for the fair.

- A: makeing — silent_e_kept_before_ing — The silent e in make should be dropped before adding -ing.
- B: makking — consonant_doubled_incorrectly — The consonant k should not be doubled when adding -ing to make.
- C: makin — final_letters_dropped — Makin drops letters and is not the standard spelling.
- D (KEY): making — correct — Making correctly drops the silent e before adding -ing.

## pssa_item_g3_moy_conv_d211_word_choice

EC: E03.D.2.1.1
Target: word or phrase choice for effect / word_choice_for_effect
The puppy ___ across the yard, kicking up dust as it hurried to greet Maya.

- A (KEY): raced — correct — Raced best matches the puppy hurrying and kicking up dust.
- B: went — overly_general_word_choice — Went is too general to show the puppy's quick movement.
- C: strolled — too_slow_for_context — Strolled suggests slow walking, which conflicts with hurried.
- D: proceeded — awkwardly_formal_register — Proceeded is too formal and does not fit the lively sentence.
