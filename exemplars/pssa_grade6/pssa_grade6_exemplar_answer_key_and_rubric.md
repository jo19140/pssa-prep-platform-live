# PSSA Grade 6 ELA — Answer Key & Rubric (Exemplar)

> Backend / educator file. Not part of the student preview.

## Question 1 — Multiple choice (E06.B-K.1.1.2, central idea)

- **Correct answer:** C — "Beavers reshape the land by building dams, which help water, soil, and wildlife."
- **correctIndex:** 2 (not position A — answer-position audit OK)

### Distractor rationales

- **A — "Beavers were hunted for their fur until they nearly disappeared."** Incorrect. A true detail drawn from paragraph 1, but it is background/setup, not what the passage as a whole is about. Tests whether the student mistakes an opening detail for the main idea.
- **B — "Calm beaver ponds attract frogs, fish, insects, and birds."** Incorrect. A real supporting detail from paragraph 4 that illustrates *one* benefit of beaver ponds; it is too narrow to be the central idea. Tests detail-vs-central-idea discrimination.
- **C — CORRECT.** States the idea developed across every paragraph: dams create ponds, which reshape water flow, protect soil, and support wildlife.
- **D — "Beavers matter mainly because they help communities save money on repairs."** Incorrect, and deliberately tempting. It draws on the final paragraph ("works for free," "rather than sending in machines and workers"), so a student who fixes on the ending may pick it. But cost savings is a minor closing point, not the central idea developed across the text. Tests whether the student can distinguish the main idea from a memorable final detail. (Note: no absolute language like "never" or "only" — the distractor is wrong by emphasis, not by being obviously extreme.)

## Question 2 — Text-Dependent Analysis (E06.E.1.1.2, develop analysis with evidence)

- **Expected claim:** The author develops the "ecosystem engineer" idea by showing the beaver as an agent that actively reshapes its environment — its dam-building creates a pond that changes how water, soil, and wildlife behave across the landscape.

- **Acceptable evidence (text-based):**
  - dam built from "branches, mud, and stones" forming a pond
  - pond "acts like a brake," slowing water and reducing floods downstream
  - land near a pond "stays green" during a dry summer
  - scientists "counted far more kinds of animals" where beavers returned
  - "a crowded apartment building for wildlife"
  - communities building simple "starter dams" to invite beavers

- **Explanation criteria:** The response must *connect* evidence to the engineer idea — explaining that each example shows the beaver changing the land — rather than listing facts or summarizing.

### Scoring notes (4/3/2/1)

- **4:** Clear claim about the beaver as an agent of change; multiple relevant pieces of text evidence; each tied explicitly to the engineer idea; organized and coherent.
- **3:** Clear claim; some relevant evidence; mostly connected to the engineer idea; minor gaps.
- **2:** Vague or partial claim; limited evidence; weak or missing link between evidence and idea; may drift into summary.
- **1:** No real claim; little or no text evidence, or pure summary/copying; does not address the engineer idea.

### Common weak responses
- Summarizing the passage without addressing the "engineer" idea.
- Listing animals without linking them to the beaver's role.
- Relying only on the title.

### Copied-text / off-topic handling
- **Copied text:** A response copied verbatim from the passage with no analysis scores **1**.
- **Off-topic:** Responses that do not address the passage or the engineer idea (e.g., beavers as pets) receive **no credit**.
