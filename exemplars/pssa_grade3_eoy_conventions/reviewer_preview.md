## pssa_item_g3_eoy_conv_d112_plurals

EC: E03.D.1.1.2
Target: regular and irregular plural nouns / irregular_plural_nouns
The farmer counted six ___ beside the pond.

- A (KEY): geese — correct — Geese is the correct irregular plural form of goose.
- B: gooses — regular_s_added_to_irregular_noun — Gooses incorrectly adds regular -s to an irregular noun.
- C: goose — singular_used_where_plural_needed — Goose is singular, but the sentence names six animals.
- D: geeses — extra_s_on_irregular_plural — Geeses adds an extra ending to the already-plural word geese.

## pssa_item_g3_eoy_conv_d113_abstract_noun

EC: E03.D.1.1.3
Target: abstract nouns / abstract_nouns
The children showed great ___ when they shared their snacks.

- A: kind — adjective_used_where_abstract_noun_needed — Kind is an adjective, but the sentence needs a noun that names the quality.
- B (KEY): kindness — correct — Kindness is an abstract noun that names the quality the children showed.
- C: kindly — adverb_used_where_abstract_noun_needed — Kindly is an adverb, not the noun needed after showed great.
- D: kinder — comparative_used_where_abstract_noun_needed — Kinder compares qualities, but it is not the noun form needed here.

## pssa_item_g3_eoy_conv_d116_pronoun_agreement

EC: E03.D.1.1.6
Target: pronoun-antecedent agreement / pronoun_antecedent_agreement
The two hikers packed ___ own water bottles.

- A (KEY): their — correct — Their is a possessive pronoun that agrees with the plural antecedent two hikers.
- B: his — singular_pronoun_with_plural_antecedent — His is singular, but two hikers is plural.
- C: they — subject_pronoun_used_where_possessive_needed — They is a subject pronoun, but the sentence needs a possessive before own.
- D: our — first_person_pronoun_mismatch — Our does not match the third-person antecedent two hikers.

## pssa_item_g3_eoy_conv_d117_comparative

EC: E03.D.1.1.7
Target: comparative and superlative adjectives / comparative_superlative
Of the three kittens, the gray one is the ___.

- A: smaller — comparative_used_for_three_or_more — Smaller compares two things, but the sentence compares three kittens.
- B: more small — more_used_with_short_adjective — More small is not the standard comparative or superlative form for the short adjective small.
- C (KEY): smallest — correct — Smallest is the superlative form used when comparing three or more.
- D: most small — most_used_with_short_adjective — Most small is not the standard superlative form for the short adjective small.

## pssa_item_g3_eoy_conv_d119_sentence_formation

EC: E03.D.1.1.9
Target: produce compound and complex sentences / sentence_formation
The sky grew dark, ___

- A (KEY): and the wind began to blow. — correct — The conjunction and joins two complete ideas with the comma already in place.
- B: the wind began to blow. — comma_splice_without_conjunction — This joins two complete sentences with only a comma.
- C: and the wind blowing hard. — fragment_without_complete_verb — Blowing has no helping verb, so the second part is not a complete idea.
- D: and the wind blew hard the trees bent. — run_on_two_complete_ideas — This adds another complete idea without punctuation to separate it.

## pssa_item_g3_eoy_conv_d122_address_commas

EC: E03.D.1.2.2
Target: commas in addresses / commas_in_addresses
My aunt's new house is in ___.

- A: Tampa Florida — missing_comma_between_city_and_state — A comma is needed between the city and state.
- B: Tampa Florida, — comma_after_state_instead_of_between — This places a comma after the state but still misses the comma between city and state.
- C: Tampa, Florida, — extra_comma_after_state_at_sentence_end — The comma between city and state is correct, but no extra comma is needed before the sentence period.
- D (KEY): Tampa, Florida — correct — A comma correctly separates the city Tampa from the state Florida.

## pssa_item_g3_eoy_conv_d123_dialogue

EC: E03.D.1.2.3
Target: quotation marks and commas in dialogue / dialogue_punctuation
Lily smiled and said, ___

- A: Let's go to the park. — missing_quotation_marks — The spoken words need quotation marks.
- B (KEY): "Let's go to the park." — correct — The spoken words are capitalized and the period is inside the quotation marks.
- C: "let's go to the park." — quotation_starts_with_lowercase_word — The first word of the quotation should be capitalized.
- D: "Let's go to the park". — period_outside_closing_quotation_mark — The period should be inside the closing quotation mark.

## pssa_item_g3_eoy_conv_d124_possessives

EC: E03.D.1.2.4
Target: form and use possessives / possessives
All three ___ kites were tangled in the tree.

- A: girls — plural_without_apostrophe — Girls is plural but does not show ownership.
- B: girl's — singular_possessive_for_plural_owners — Girl's shows one owner, but all three girls own the kites.
- C (KEY): girls' — correct — Girls' is the plural possessive form for more than one girl.
- D: girls's — extra_s_after_plural_possessive — Girls's incorrectly adds another s after the plural possessive.

## pssa_item_g3_eoy_conv_d126_spelling

EC: E03.D.1.2.6
Target: spelling patterns and generalizations / spelling_ending_rules
The children were ___ across the open field.

- A: runing — final_consonant_not_doubled_before_ing — Running needs a doubled n before adding -ing.
- B: runnning — consonant_doubled_too_many_times — Runnning has too many n letters.
- C: runnig — letters_dropped_from_ing_ending — Runnig drops a letter from the -ing ending.
- D (KEY): running — correct — Running correctly doubles the final consonant before -ing.
