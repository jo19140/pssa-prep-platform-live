Design notes · parent voice visibility

  • The audio IS the product for parents. A parent who hears their kid sound out "disappeared" after two tries is emotionally hooked in a way no dashboard can match. The hero CTA is "listen to today's session." Everything else is supporting evidence.
  • AI summary in the buddy's voice. Reuses the character — the same persona that teaches the kid summarizes for the parent. Continuity matters; the parent should feel they're hearing from "the teacher," not "the analytics tool."
  • Markers, not raw waveform. The timeline highlights the 2–3 moments worth a listen. A parent isn't going to scrub 16 minutes of audio — but they'll happily click a 40-second clip labeled "she figured out 'disappeared' on her own."
  • Words she asked about > words she got wrong. Asking is a growth behavior. Reframing "vocab gaps" as "words she had the curiosity to ask about" changes the parent's lens — they see their kid as engaged, not deficient.
  • "Something to talk about at dinner." The product extends into the home as a conversation prompt, not homework. The script is provided so parents don't have to figure out what to say.
  • Privacy explicit, not hidden in TOS. Voice data is uniquely sensitive — and kids' voices double so. Surface the controls on this page, not buried in settings. Trust here is the whole product.
  • Spanish toggle on every text block. Bilingual support isn't a single toggle in settings — it's per-component, so parents can read the dashboard in English and the buddy's summary in Spanish if they want. Builds toward the Tier 4 Spanish-mode roadmap item.
