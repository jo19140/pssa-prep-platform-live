Design notes · speed drill mode

  • Methodology source. 1-minute timed oral reading with WCPM measurement is the exact instructional pattern from the uploaded Blevins phonics teaching guide. Error classification (correct / self-fixed / missed) mirrors the manual's specification.
  • Content is phonogram-targeted, not random. Today's drill is r-controlled because that's Maya's current developing area (visible on her phonogram mastery grid). The autopilot picks the focus; the drill content auto-generates from the targeted phonogram family using platform-owned word lists.
  • Real-time scoring during the drill. Voice transcription runs continuously; the live counter ("11 read · 9 correct · 1 self-fixed · 1 missed") gives the kid in-progress feedback without breaking concentration. Already-read words gray out so the kid keeps moving forward.
  • Self-corrected counts as a win. Pedagogically important — kids who notice their own mistakes are demonstrating metacognition. The amber color and "fixed yourself" label honor that, instead of marking them wrong.
  • "Words we'll try again tomorrow." The missed words go into a personal-rotation pool, not a punishment list. Buddy's commentary ("both have a soft second sound") names the pattern, building the kid's awareness without making them feel deficient.
  • Progress chart over the previous 8 runs. Kids find it highly motivating to watch their own line go up (verbatim from the phonics teaching guide). This is the visible payoff of the daily 1-minute commitment.
  • Voice-first end to end. The kid reads aloud, the buddy listens. Same TTS/STT infrastructure as voice-practice — no new platform muscle required.
