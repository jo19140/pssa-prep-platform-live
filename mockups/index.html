The literacy layer · autopilot mode

Six mockups exploring how the platform reaches students who can't read at grade level and the adults around them — with the AI running autonomously by default. Teachers monitor and can override; parents get a warm summary, no homework. Sample student throughout: Maya Chen, Grade 5, reading ~Grade 3 level. Decoding is fine; vocabulary and comprehension are the gaps.

Locked product calls reflected in these mockups

  • Autopilot by default. Diagnostic auto-runs, plan auto-applies, sessions auto-progress, parent updates auto-send. Teacher controls are overrides, not approvals.
  • Educator framing: augmentation, not replacement. "The program handles the work so you focus on the kid." Never "sit back and let the AI teach."
  • 6-strand literacy model. Phonemic Awareness · Decoding (phonograms + 6 syllable types) · Morphology · Fluency · Vocabulary · Comprehension. "Striving readers" — not "struggling."
  • Ehri phase as the diagnostic headline. Lexile is a supporting signal; Ehri phase placement (Pre-alphabetic → Partial → Full → Consolidated) is what drives instruction.
  • Voice-first wedge. The program literally speaks to the kid. Kid talks back. Buddy character with idle/listening/speaking/confused states. Open-ended answers LLM-graded for gist.
  • Phonogram-aware scaffolding. When a kid is stuck, Buddy splits the word using the six syllable types, highlighting phonogram chunks the kid already owns. Implicit consolidated-alphabetic instruction.
  • Dialect & L1 awareness. Cross-language sound-spelling transfer (ENG ↔ Spanish/Vietnamese/Hmong/Cantonese) and AAE recognition. Family opts in via skip-able onboarding. Never inferred from name or audio. No race questions.
  • PSSA is a graduation, not an entry. Literacy layer is the foundation; PSSA prep is what happens once a student is at level.
  • Parent-pay is a first-class market. The parent dashboard has no teacher in the loop. A school deployment of the same product layers the teacher monitor on top.
