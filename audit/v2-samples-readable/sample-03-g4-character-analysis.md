## Hook

Have you ever read a story and felt like you really knew the characters, as if they were your friends? Understanding characters in a story is like getting to know new friends. We learn about them through their actions, words, and how they interact with others. Today, we'll explore how to dive deep into character analysis, making stories more engaging and relatable.

## Explanation (1841 chars)

Character analysis involves examining characters to understand their personalities, motivations, and roles in the story. Characters are revealed through actions, dialogue, and interactions. For example, a character who helps others might be seen as kind. Conversely, a character who lies might be seen as untrustworthy.

To analyze a character, identify key actions and ask why they acted that way. Consider what their words reveal about their thoughts and feelings. Observe how other characters react to them. Are they admired or misunderstood? These reactions offer clues about the character's role.

Look for changes in the character over time. Do they learn a lesson or change behavior? This growth can be crucial to understanding the story's theme. By piecing together these elements, you can form a comprehensive picture of the character, making the story more meaningful and enjoyable. ## Worked example (1025 chars)

Let's analyze a character from a short passage. Imagine a story where a character named Lily is always seen helping her classmates with their homework. One day, she stays late after school to tutor a friend who is struggling. Through these actions, we can infer that Lily is generous and values helping others.

Now, consider Lily's dialogue. She often says things like, "I'm happy to help," or "Let's figure this out together." Her words reinforce her actions, showing she genuinely enjoys assisting others. Additionally, her classmates often thank her and express gratitude, indicating that they appreciate her kindness and rely on her support.

Finally, observe any changes in Lily. Perhaps she starts the story shy and reserved but becomes more confident as she helps others. This growth suggests that helping others also helps her grow personally. By examining Lily's actions, words, and development, we gain a deeper understanding of her character and the story's message about the importance of kindness and community.

## Common errors

- Students may focus only on dialogue without considering actions.
- Some students might overlook how other characters' reactions contribute to understanding a character.
- Students might ignore character development over time.

## Sentence frames

- The character's actions suggest that they are...
- When the character says..., it reveals that they...
- Other characters react to them by..., which shows...

## Success criteria

- Identify key actions and dialogue that reveal character traits.
- Explain how other characters' reactions contribute to understanding the character.
- Describe any changes in the character and what they signify about the story's theme.

**Question**: What does the character's action of helping classmates reveal about them?

- He is caring. **← CORRECT**
- He is lazy.

**Why right**: Jake's actions of helping the lost puppy show he is caring and determined.

**Distractor rationales**:
- _He is selfish._: This choice contradicts his actions of helping others without expecting anything in return.
