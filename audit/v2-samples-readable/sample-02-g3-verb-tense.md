## Hook

Imagine telling a story about your weekend, but you mix up when things happened. You might say, "I will go to the park yesterday," which sounds confusing! Verb tenses help us know when actions happen. Today, we'll learn how to use verb tenses correctly so our stories and sentences make sense.

## Explanation (1839 chars)

Verb tenses are crucial in English because they tell us when an action takes place. There are three main tenses: past, present, and future. The past tense describes actions that have already happened, like "I walked to school." The present tense describes actions happening now, such as "I am walking to school." The future tense describes actions that will happen, like "I will walk to school." Using the wrong tense can lead to misunderstandings. For example, if you say, "I eat dinner last night," it sounds like you are eating dinner now, which is confusing. In storytelling, verb tenses help create a clear timeline of events. If you mix them up, your story might not make sense. In instructions, using the wrong tense can lead to mistakes. For instance, "You will mix the ingredients" instead of "You mix the ingredients" can confuse someone following a recipe. Understanding verb tenses helps us communicate clearly and accurately in both writing and speaking. Let's practice identifying and using the correct verb tenses to improve our communication skills! We'll explore different scenarios and see how changing the verb tense changes the meaning of a sentence. This will help us become more confident in using verb tenses correctly in our writing. ## Worked example (860 chars)

Let's look at an example of how verb tenses work in a story. Imagine a story about a boy named Tim. In the past tense, we might say, "Tim played soccer yesterday." This tells us that the action happened in the past. If we use the present tense, it becomes "Tim plays soccer every day," indicating that it's a regular action happening now. For the future tense, we could say, "Tim will play soccer tomorrow," showing that the action will happen later. Notice how the verb changes to match the time of the action. If we mix up the tenses, like saying "Tim will played soccer yesterday," it becomes confusing because the time frame is unclear. By practicing with different sentences, we can learn to choose the correct verb tense and make our writing clear and precise. This understanding helps us tell stories and give instructions that others can easily follow.

## Common errors

- Mixing up past and present tense in a sentence.
- Using future tense when describing past events.
- Confusing present continuous with simple present.

## Sentence frames

- Yesterday, I [verb] to the store.
- Today, I [verb] my homework.
- Tomorrow, I will [verb] to the park.
