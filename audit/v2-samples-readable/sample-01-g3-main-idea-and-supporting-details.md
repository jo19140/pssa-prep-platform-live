## Hook

Imagine you're a detective trying to solve a mystery. Each story or article is like a case, and the main idea is the big clue that helps you understand what it's all about. Just like a detective gathers evidence, you'll find supporting details that help explain the main idea. Today, we'll learn how to uncover these clues in any text!

## Explanation (1980 chars)

The main idea of a text is the central point or the most important thing the author wants you to know. Think of it as the heart of the story or article. Supporting details are the pieces of information that help explain or prove the main idea. They are like the branches that grow from the main idea, providing more information and context.

To find the main idea, ask yourself, "What is this text mostly about?" Look for repeated ideas or themes. Sometimes, the main idea is stated directly, often in the first or last sentence of a paragraph. Other times, you need to infer it by considering all the details together.

Supporting details can be facts, examples, or descriptions that help you understand the main idea better. They answer questions like "Who? What? When? Where? Why? and How?" These details are crucial because they give you the evidence you need to understand and explain the main idea.

For example, if you're reading a passage about how bees make honey, the main idea might be "Bees play an essential role in producing honey." Supporting details could include descriptions of how bees collect nectar, the process of turning nectar into honey, and the importance of honey in ecosystems.

By identifying the main idea and supporting details, you can better understand and remember what you read. This skill is essential for reading comprehension and will help you in all subjects. For example, students should name the exact clue, connect it to the question, and explain why it proves the answer. A common mistake is choosing a detail that sounds related but does not actually prove the idea. Strong work uses the standard's language and shows the reasoning step by step. ## Worked example (950 chars)

Let's look at a short passage:

"Dogs are known as man's best friend for many reasons. They are loyal and protective, often guarding their owners and homes. Dogs also provide companionship and can be trained to perform various tasks, such as fetching items or guiding the visually impaired."

First, identify the main idea. Ask yourself, "What is this passage mostly about?" The main idea here is that dogs are valuable companions to humans.

Next, find the supporting details. These are the facts and examples that explain why dogs are considered man's best friend. In this passage, the supporting details include:
- Dogs are loyal and protective.
- They guard their owners and homes.
- They provide companionship.
- They can be trained to perform tasks.

By connecting these details to the main idea, you can see how they support the central point of the passage. This process helps you understand the text more deeply and remember the information.

## Common errors

- Confusing supporting details with the main idea.
- Overlooking the main idea when it's not directly stated.
- Focusing on minor details instead of key points.

## Sentence frames

- The main idea of the passage is ____.
- A supporting detail that explains the main idea is ____.
- This detail supports the main idea because ____.

## Success criteria

- I can identify the main idea of a passage.
- I can find supporting details that explain the main idea.
- I can explain how the details support the main idea.
