These are starting concepts to react to, not finished logos. The wordmark itself is the same across all four; what differs is whether (and how) a separate mark accompanies it. Each SVG saved alongside this file is hand-editable in Figma, Illustrator, or a designer's tool of choice.

Honest notes

  • These are direction sketches I made in code, not finished logos. They show intent — color palette, structure, the relationship between mark and wordmark — but every one of them would benefit from a real designer's hand to perfect the typography, balance, and weight.
  • If you want to refine without hiring a designer immediately, run your favorite concept through Figma's community templates or feed the SVG to Midjourney/ChatGPT with "polish this brand mark, keep the same composition, refine the typography."
  • The color palette across all four is intentionally consistent: indigo (#1e1b4b) for gravitas, amber (#f59e0b) for warmth, slate (#94a3b8) for support. Same palette appears throughout the product mockups.
  • The wordmark in all four uses Georgia as a placeholder. Production should use Cormorant Garamond, Cardo, or a custom commission. The serif is doing the "classical educational pedigree" work; don't lose it.
  • My personal favorite is Concept 3 (Orbit) — it ties the brand directly to the planet-subject product architecture you sketched. Concept 1 (wordmark only) is the safest fallback if you want maximum flexibility.
