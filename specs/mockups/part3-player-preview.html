Design notes (adult-only) — grounded in the voice pilot, 158 clips / 6 kids.
• Harper never says “wrong” and never echoes a transcript. The recognizer returned “K” / “Qué?” for a soft reader who read correctly — so “you said ___” is unsafe. Re-teach is rule-anchored from the known target, not the heard audio.
• Isolated words default to harper_retry_only — Harper listens and re-prompts, but does not make a mastery decision from ASR at this tier (isolated false-negative ~36%, up to 72% for a soft reader). No autonomous reject.
• A “miss” is gentle and bounded: attempt 1 → “read it once more”; repeat → rule re-teach (silent-e); still stuck → Harper models it, marks assisted, moves on. A struggling reader is never stuck or scolded.
• Line 4 (silly words) = read-and-self-confirm, never ASR-scored (pseudowords over-normalize).
• Every attempt emits evidence for the teacher view; nothing the child sees says “fail.”
