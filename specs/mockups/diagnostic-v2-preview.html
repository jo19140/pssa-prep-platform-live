Implementation notes for Codex
Hidden from student
No student metadata
Student never sees phase labels, item numbers, score bars, or correctness feedback during scored diagnostic items.
Routing ≠ placement
Short item sets can route up/down. Final placement requires ≥5 scored items per phase being considered, including real-word + pseudoword evidence for decoding.
Latency hidden
Response latency is recorded on every decoding item but never shown to the student. 5s flags delayed (feeds fluency strand and tutor view); 10s scores as no-attempt. Placement uses accuracy alone; latency feeds the separate fluency strand.
Within-strand ceiling
4 consecutive misses within a strand stops upward probing in that strand. The diagnostic moves on to the next strand without skipping.
Speech safety
Low-confidence ASR, unclear audio, repeated attempts, or dialect ambiguity should flag the item rather than silently score it as wrong. Flagged items are excluded from placement and reported in the audio-quality metric.
Probe calibration
Fluency and reading-comp passages are pre-calibrated to phase bands and stored as approved Passage rows. Routing picks a starting band from decoding evidence and may step one band up or down based on accuracy.
Conservative claims
Until validated norms exist, use "priority," "relative strength," and "on this diagnostic," not grade-level claims. Reserve validation language for a future spec that ships actual NCII-aligned evidence.
