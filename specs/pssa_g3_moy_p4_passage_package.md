## 1. Topic & title

Three students and their teacher are one afternoon away from the spring play when the painted castle backdrop tears in half. Jada wants to cancel, Marcus insists on repainting the exact castle, and Priya proposes a simpler, new design. By listening to one another and combining their different strengths — Priya designs, Marcus builds and paints, Jada organizes — they build something that works better than the original. Central message (**inferred**): **listening to one another and combining everyone's strengths can solve a problem**, better than giving up or clinging to one plan. Title: **The Last Rehearsal.**

## 2. Passage

**CHARACTERS**
MS. REYES, the drama teacher
JADA, a student who likes everything planned
MARCUS, the student who painted the castle backdrop
PRIYA, a student who is quiet but full of ideas

**SETTING:** The stage of the school auditorium, the afternoon before the spring play. A tall painted castle stands at the back of the stage. Folding chairs, a ladder, and open buckets of paint sit off to one side.

---

**Scene 1: The Discovery**

*(The stage lights are on. JADA holds a script and checks items off a list. MARCUS climbs down a ladder, looking proud of his work. PRIYA sits on the floor, drawing in a notebook.)*

JADA: One more rehearsal and we are ready. The show is tomorrow night, and everything is perfect.

MARCUS: *(patting the painted castle)* It should be. I spent three weeks on this castle. Every stone, every window, I painted by hand.

PRIYA: It does look real, Marcus. It looks like you could walk right through the gate.

*(A sudden gust blows in through a propped-open door. The tall castle leans, then wobbles. With a loud RIP, the painted cloth tears straight down the middle and folds to the floor in two ragged pieces.)*

JADA: *(gasping)* No. No, no, no.

MARCUS: *(running over)* My castle! *(He lifts a torn corner and stares at the rip.)* It's ruined. Three weeks, gone in one second.

PRIYA: *(quietly)* Oh no.

JADA: *(pacing frantically back and forth)* The show is tomorrow! We cannot put on a play with a broken castle. The whole audience will laugh. I already have butterflies in my stomach just thinking about it.

MARCUS: *(still gripping the torn cloth)* Maybe she's right. What is a castle play with no castle?

MS. REYES: *(hurrying in)* I heard a crash all the way down the hall. *(She sees the torn backdrop and stops short.)* Oh. That is a big tear.

JADA: We should cancel, Ms. Reyes. We have to. There is no time left to fix something that size.

MS. REYES: Let's not decide that yet. You three have good heads on your shoulders. Talk it through before you give up.

*(She steps to the side of the stage and waits. The three students stand in a row, looking at the ruined castle.)*

**Scene 2: Three Plans**

MARCUS: I can repaint it. I'll start right now and paint all night if I have to.

JADA: All night? Marcus, it took you three whole weeks the first time.

MARCUS: Then I'll paint faster. *(He grabs a brush and pries the lid off a paint bucket.)* If I don't, we're back to square one.

JADA: We are *already* back to square one. That is exactly why we should stop. A torn castle on stage is worse than no play at all.

PRIYA: *(standing up slowly)* Maybe... maybe it doesn't have to be the same castle.

MARCUS: *(frowning)* What do you mean? The whole story happens at a castle. We need a castle.

PRIYA: We need the audience to *think* castle. That's different. *(She opens her notebook and holds up a sketch.)* What if we don't copy the old one? What if we build something new — together?

JADA: *(leaning in)* Like what?

PRIYA: We hang the plain dark curtain that's already in the closet. Then we cut a few simple shapes out of cardboard — one tall tower, one gate, one moon — and pin them to the curtain. We aim the stage lights low so the shapes throw long shadows. The audience fills in the rest with their imagination.

MARCUS: *(shaking his head)* It won't look real. Mine looked real. You could see every brick.

PRIYA: Yours *was* beautiful, Marcus. Truly. But the beautiful one broke. This one would be quick, and from the seats it would still feel like a castle at night.

MARCUS: *(crossing his arms)* The whole front row will see it is just cardboard.

PRIYA: The front row will be watching the actors, not the wall. A set is supposed to help the story, not steal it.

JADA: She's right. Last spring, nobody remembered the scenery — they remembered the play. *(softly)* And we don't have three weeks, Marcus. We have one afternoon.

*(MARCUS looks at the torn cloth on the floor, then at Priya's sketch. He is quiet for a long moment. He sets the brush down on the bucket.)*

MARCUS: *(slowly)* ...Okay. Show me what you mean.

*(PRIYA's face lights up. JADA lets out a long, slow breath, and her shoulders drop.)*

**Scene 3: The Last Rehearsal**

*(Time has passed. The dark curtain now hangs across the back of the stage. MARCUS kneels, painting a tall cardboard tower. JADA pins a paper moon into place and checks her list. PRIYA crouches by the lights.)*

JADA: Tower — check. Gate — check. Moon — check. *(She steps back.)* Marcus, your tower looks wonderful.

MARCUS: *(surprised)* It's just cardboard and a little paint. Twenty minutes of work, not three weeks.

PRIYA: But you knew exactly how to shape it and where the shadows would fall. I could never paint a tower like that. *(She moves to the light switches.)* Ready? Everybody watch.

*(She turns the stage lights down low. The simple cut-out shapes throw tall, soft shadows across the dark curtain. All at once, the stage looks like a castle standing in the moonlight.)*

JADA: *(whispering)* It's... it's better. It honestly looks like a real night at a real castle.

MARCUS: *(staring up at the shadows)* Huh. The shadows do half the work for us.

PRIYA: That's the trick. Less stuff, more imagination.

JADA: *(checking her list one more time)* And it all fits. Props by the door, lights set, and everyone knows exactly where to stand.

MS. REYES: *(returning, looking up at the stage)* Oh, my. *(She turns to the three of them.)* An hour ago you had a torn cloth on the floor and three different plans. Now look at this.

JADA: We almost canceled the whole show.

MARCUS: And I almost painted all night for nothing.

PRIYA: *(grinning)* Good thing we each knew how to help.

*(They laugh. MS. REYES claps her hands together twice.)*

MS. REYES: Places, everyone. Let's take it from the top — one last rehearsal before tomorrow.

*(JADA picks up her script, no longer frantic. MARCUS wipes his hands and steps onto the stage. PRIYA dims the lights, and the shadow castle rises softly behind them.)*

JADA: *(smiling)* Ready when you are.

*(The stage lights glow. The three friends take their places as the curtain slowly begins to rise.)*

## 4. Scene / structure map

- **Scene 1 — The Discovery:** establishes the deadline ("show is tomorrow") and each character; the **explicit event** (the castle backdrop tears down the middle); first motivations surface (Jada → cancel; Marcus → his three weeks of work; Priya → watching, thinking).
- **Scene 2 — Three Plans:** the conflict. Marcus insists on repainting the exact castle (pride / wants it to look real); Jada argues to stop (fear it will go wrong); Priya proposes a new, simpler design and asks them to build it *together*. **Turning point:** Marcus listens and agrees ("...Okay. Show me what you mean.").
- **Scene 3 — The Last Rehearsal:** they combine **strengths** (Priya's design + Marcus's shaping/painting + Jada's organizing); the shadow castle works and looks better; the message is shown through Priya's line "Good thing we each knew how to help" and the successful result — **never stated as a moral**.
